Paneer Butter Masala Recipe

Paneer Butter Masala is a luxurious vegetarian dish featuring paneer cubes bathed in a creamy tomato-based sauce, infused with aromatic spices and finished with a touch of butter. This dish is a favorite comfort food in Indian cuisine, perfect for special occasions or a satisfying weeknight meal.

Preparation-time

  • Total: Approximately 35 minutes
  • Preparation: 15 minutes
  • Cooking: 20 minutes

Ingredients

  • 250g paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

1.

Sauté Onions:Heat 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium heat.Add 1 large finely chopped onion.Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.

2.

Add Ginger-Garlic Paste :Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste to the pan.Sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.

3.

Cook Tomato Puree: Add 2 tomatoes pureed to the pan. Cook until the oil separates from the mixture and it thickens.

4.

Spice it Up:Stir in 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, and 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala powder.Cook for 2-3 minutes.

5.

Add Paneer:Add 250g of paneer cubes to the pan. Gently mix to coat them with the masala.

6.

Introduce Cream:Pour in 1/4 cup of heavy cream. Add 1/2 teaspoon of crushed kasuri methi.Stir well to combine.

7.

Simmer and Season:Let the paneer butter masala simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors meld together and the sauce reaches your desired consistency.Taste and adjust the seasoning by adding salt if necessary.

8.

Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
